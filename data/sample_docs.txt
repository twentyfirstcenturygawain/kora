## Acme SaaS Platform — Enterprise Knowledge Base

### Product Overview
Acme is a cloud-based workflow automation platform designed for mid-market and enterprise companies. It connects your team's tools, data, and processes into unified, automated workflows. Acme is used by over 4,000 companies across industries including healthcare, finance, logistics, and retail.

Core products include: Acme Core (workflow builder), Acme Intelligence (AI-powered automation), Acme Connect (integrations hub), and Acme Insights (analytics and reporting).

### Getting Started
To create your account, visit app.acme.com and click "Sign Up". Enter your business email and choose a password. You'll receive a confirmation email within 2 minutes. If you don't see it, check your spam folder or request a resend from the login page.

After logging in for the first time, you'll be guided through a 5-minute setup wizard to configure your workspace, invite team members, and connect your first integration. We recommend starting with the Quick Start templates available under Templates > Featured.

For enterprise onboarding, a dedicated Customer Success Manager will be assigned within 24 hours of contract signing. Enterprise customers receive a structured 30-60-90 day onboarding plan.

### Pricing and Plans
Acme offers four plans tailored to different team sizes and needs.

Starter plan costs $29 per month and supports up to 3 users, 10,000 API calls per month, 5 active workflows, and community support only.

Growth plan costs $99 per month and supports up to 25 users, 100,000 API calls per month, unlimited workflows, priority email support, webhook support, and advanced analytics.

Business plan costs $299 per month and supports up to 100 users, 1,000,000 API calls per month, SSO, custom roles and permissions, audit logs, SLA of 99.9% uptime, and a dedicated Slack support channel.

Enterprise plan has custom pricing and includes unlimited users, unlimited API calls, custom SLAs, dedicated account manager, on-premise deployment options, custom security review, and professional services.

All plans include a 14-day free trial with no credit card required. You can upgrade, downgrade, or cancel at any time from Settings > Billing. Annual billing provides a 20% discount on all plans.

### Integrations and Connections
Acme integrates natively with over 200 tools. Key integrations include Salesforce, HubSpot, Marketo, Pipedrive for CRM; Slack, Microsoft Teams, Google Workspace for communication; Jira, Asana, Linear, Monday.com for project management; Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift, PostgreSQL for data; Stripe, QuickBooks, NetSuite for finance; and Zendesk, Intercom, Freshdesk for customer support.

To connect an integration, go to Settings > Integrations, search for the service, and click Connect. You'll be redirected through an OAuth flow. Admin permissions are required on both platforms. Some integrations require API keys instead of OAuth — these are documented at docs.acme.com/integrations.

For custom integrations, Acme provides a fully documented REST API and GraphQL API. API keys are generated from Settings > Developer. Webhook support with retry logic is available on Growth and above. Acme also supports Zapier, Make, and n8n for no-code integration building.

Rate limits: Starter is 10 requests/second, Growth is 50 requests/second, Business is 200 requests/second, Enterprise is custom.

### Workflow Automation
Workflows are built using a visual drag-and-drop editor. Each workflow has a trigger (event that starts it), conditions (optional logic), and actions (what happens). Triggers can be time-based (scheduled), event-based (a record changes), or webhook-based (external system sends data).

Acme supports conditional branching, loops, parallel execution, and error handling within workflows. You can add human approval steps, time delays, and sub-workflows. AI-powered steps powered by Acme Intelligence allow you to classify data, extract information, summarize content, and generate responses automatically within a workflow.

Workflow templates are available for common use cases including lead routing, invoice processing, employee onboarding, support ticket triage, and monthly reporting.

### Billing and Invoices
Billing occurs on the first of each month for monthly plans, or annually on the contract start date for annual plans. Payment methods accepted include all major credit cards, ACH bank transfer (US only), and wire transfer (Enterprise only).

Invoices are emailed automatically to the billing contact and are also available in Settings > Billing > Invoice History. You can add multiple billing contacts under Settings > Billing > Contacts.

If a payment fails, Acme retries three times over 7 days. You receive email notifications at each attempt. After three failures the account is suspended but all data is retained for 30 days. Reactivate by updating your payment method in Settings > Billing.

To dispute a charge contact billing@acme.com within 30 days of the invoice date with your invoice number and reason. Refunds are processed within 5-10 business days.

### Data, Security, and Compliance
Acme stores all data in AWS across three regions: us-east-1 (primary), eu-west-1 (EU customers), and ap-southeast-1 (APAC customers). Data residency can be selected during account setup or changed by contacting support.

All data is encrypted at rest using AES-256 and in transit using TLS 1.3. Encryption keys are managed using AWS KMS with customer-managed key (CMK) support available on Enterprise plans.

Acme holds the following certifications: SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, GDPR compliant, HIPAA compliant (Business and Enterprise plans only), and PCI-DSS Level 1 for payment data handling.

A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is available for all paid plans and is automatically included in Enterprise contracts. To request a DPA contact legal@acme.com.

Single Sign-On (SSO) is supported via SAML 2.0 and OIDC on Business and Enterprise plans. Supported identity providers include Okta, Azure AD, Google Workspace, OneLogin, and Ping Identity.

Role-based access control (RBAC) is available on Business and Enterprise plans. Roles include Admin, Manager, Editor, Viewer, and custom roles. Permissions can be set at the workspace, project, and individual workflow level.

### Data Export and Portability
You can export all your data at any time from Settings > Data > Export. Available export formats include JSON, CSV, and XML. Exports are delivered as a ZIP file to your registered email within 24 hours for standard exports, or within 4 hours on Business and Enterprise plans.

Automated recurring exports can be configured on Business and Enterprise plans. You can also use the API to pull data programmatically at any time.

Account deletion permanently removes all data within 30 days. A deletion confirmation report is provided upon request.

### Performance and Reliability
Acme maintains 99.9% uptime SLA on Growth and above, and 99.99% on Enterprise. Real-time status and incident history are available at status.acme.com. Subscribe to status updates via email, Slack, or SMS.

Planned maintenance windows are Sundays between 2am-4am UTC and are announced at least 72 hours in advance via email and the status page. Emergency maintenance may occur with less notice.

### Troubleshooting
If the dashboard isn't loading, try a hard refresh (Cmd+Shift+R on Mac, Ctrl+Shift+R on Windows). Clear browser cache and cookies if the problem persists. Acme supports Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. Internet Explorer is not supported.

For API errors: 400 means bad request (check your payload), 401 means invalid API key, 403 means insufficient permissions, 429 means rate limit exceeded (upgrade plan or wait for reset), 500 means server error (check status.acme.com).

Workflow failures are logged under Workflows > Logs. Each failed execution shows the exact step that failed, the error message, and input/output data for debugging. You can retry failed executions manually or configure automatic retries (up to 3 times with exponential backoff).
